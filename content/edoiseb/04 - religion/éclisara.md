- their word for the visions: eclipses. adherents: also eclipses. one word. the visions and the worshippers are the same noun — we are all eclipses of what we should have been. the grammar is the theology
- pre-çintálhi: quiet. contemplative. no missionary impulse. rural, frontier, the small towns and the cold places. no temples worth the word — practiced at home, at hearths, in small gatherings. the unflashy religion. underestimated
- practice: rites of remembering. marking-what-was-lost. naming the diminishments. a culture of stoic grief and quiet daily honoring of an unwitnessed fall. we walk on what we were
- post-çintálhi reframing: "the reversion is only a mild shape of what we would have been had we not lost ourselves to cosmos bullshit. we could have been so much more. we lost so much progress"
- the saddest reframing. grief that refuses even consolation. yes, the visions came back. no, they didn't come back enough. we are still less than we should have been, and now so are they. éclisaras look at çintalhenses and are sad they aren't more
- not disdain. not awe. not fear. grief. çintalhenses are closer to the lost shape than the unconverted are — which is sad, not sacred. a çintalhense in Éclisara is in a lonely place: they are simultaneously the holier thing and a failure of holiness
- politically: unfocused. doesn't crusade. doesn't ascend. its sadness is too internal to be weaponized. the state can't co-opt grief that won't say anything good about the new world
- vow's frontier family — Éclisara descent. the stoic-hard variant: we are diminished, therefore we must be hard, because softness gets the diminished killed. the religion's bones, secularized into doctrine. they may not have practiced. they didn't need to. the hardness was the practice
- Bell — Éclisara descent. the tender-mourner variant: we are diminished, therefore we must be gentle, because diminished things deserve gentleness. the same religion, the opposite practice. the grief without the hardness
- that's the real split between vow and bell. same family. same religious bones. one took the iron half. the other took the soft. vow's vow against being powerless and bell's quiet feeding of strangers are the same religion, lived in opposite directions

### RELIGION • ÉCLISARA • THE REMEMBERING

- no journey. no destination. no rebirth. no ascension. no afterlife in any positive sense
- the soul fades. like humanity faded from its true shape. death is diminishment continuing
- and yet — the religion isn't despair. it's a practice
- the soul continues only insofar as the people who knew you remember you. when nobody holds you anymore, you are gone. completely. that is the end. there is no further
- the religion's central act is the remembering. the holding of others. eating meals named for the dead. saying the names. funerals as the formal transfer of the carrying — the people who held the dead while alive pass that holding to the next generation. you are buried in other people. literal burial is incidental
- the worst death is the unwitnessed one. dying with no one to carry you. that is the éclipse's deepest fear — and the religion's whole practice exists to spare as many people as possible from it
- conversion is no soteriological event. doesn't matter. çintalhenses are also fading from a fading shape, also carried by those who knew them, also will end. we are all eclipses. the doctrine is in the grammar
- the post-çintálhi reframing reads straight from this: "the reversion is only a mild shape" = the visions came back diminished, like everything. "we could have been so much more" = grief never going to be consoled. éclisara is the only religion whose theology cannot be helped by the post-çintálhi facts. vitravora was vindicated. căntalume was fulfilled. éclisara just got more to grieve
- practices: the saying of names. ledgers kept not as records but as holdings. shared meals where one seat is left for those being remembered. funerals where the carrying is named, witnessed, transferred. a quiet, ritualized refusal to let people end
