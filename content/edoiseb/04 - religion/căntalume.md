### RELIGION • CĂNTALUME • THE LIGHT-SHAPE

- you get one life. one. no rebirth, no second chances
- across that life, you build a light-shape inside the body. song. ritual. right action. liturgy. the singing of the light isn't metaphor — it's the actual technique. you accumulate brightness over years
- at death, the light-shape either dissolves into the universal radiance (ascension) or fades incomplete
- failure isn't damnation. just fading. you simply weren't bright enough. you were. now you aren't. most people fade. ascension is rare
- conversion = the light made flesh. mid-life. the soul's light-shape becomes bright enough that it starts manifesting in the body itself. transformation is the ascent becoming visible while still alive
- by căntalume's own logic, [[çintalhenses]] are people whose souls worked. the proof that ascension is real. the visible, undeniable evidence
- the post-[[çintálhi]] reframing reads straight from this: "we wronged the [[çintalhenses|luminads]]" = we mistreated people whose light-shapes had succeeded. "the [[the revolution (90PÇ)|revolution]] is the gods' just punishment" = we failed to recognize the realized and now they rule us, properly. "we can only hope to ascend as they did" = we must build brightly enough to become visible
- the state's co-option is partial-truth. it keeps the adoration half. strips the penitential half. the state needs behold the holy ones, not we hurt them on their way up and we owe them everything
- moderate [[çintalhenses]] raised căntalume often grieve quietly the cost their own ascension imposed on the unconverted who couldn't see them clearly. they did the thing the religion said was possible. they don't want it to have cost what it cost
- practices: liturgical song (the light-shape built audibly). light-rituals. vertical pilgrimage (geography mirroring the rise). public naming of obstacles to ascent — the religion has confession, but the confession is of dimming, not of sin
- death rites are about witnessing the light-shape at the moment of release. the dying are sung to. lit. mourned-or-celebrated depending on how bright the shape seems. a beautiful dying is one the religion can see go
