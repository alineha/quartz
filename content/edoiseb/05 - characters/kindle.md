- unconverted, jester, knight-like figure. uses swords for performance (but does know how to fight)
- acts mix comedy with swordplay — clown-like behavior that suddenly turns into a wow-inducing display of skill. funny and unsettling
- makes people happy out of duty, not kindness. hates herself
- performs kindness she doesn't feel
- knight-coded → she serves the audience. owes them joy and can never pay the debt
- swords are practical (her only weapon) and symbolic (relic, discipline, the only thing she's actually good at & also a way to hurt herself through endless training)

#### BACKSTORY

- trained pre-revolution in "[[a vârsălhe]]" - a small, traditional swordsmanship discipline. predates electricity. tied to a specific cultural/regional tradition
- kindle was born in the most traditional [[a vârsălhe]] family, a pre-revolution aristocrat → she trained intensely. exceptional student.
- kindle's family - the [[qervâdle|qervâdles]] - were the pre-revolution state's official executioners of condemned/"gone" çintalhenses.
- her family's name is still known among older [[çintalhenses]]. some remember. kindle changed her surname. lives in terror of someone recognizing her face
- post-revolution: parents imprisoned. she lives like they're gone
- kindle was trained as its next instrument. at 16–17 she believed her teachers completely. they were everything to her
- during the revolution, the [[a vârsălhe|vârsalhos]] fought. she fought. her one and only killing was a mercy:
  - wounded [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]], dying slowly. transformation partway through (still mostly human-looking, just starting to change). young. even younger than kindle
  - they asked her to end it. she did it cleanly and professionally
  - she didn't kill in hatred. she killed in something close to caring.
  - this is the worst part: it wasn't a deviation from her training. she did for once the exact thing her bloodline did coldly for the state, and told herself the same thing they told themselves: that the clean end was a kindness.
  - afterward she threw up. as far as she knows, nobody saw
- [[a vârsălhe]] was crushed after the revolution. members killed, scattered and imprisoned.
- she survived because her teachers smuggled her out of [[a vârsălhe|vârsălhe]] persecution. they chose her specifically, as she was both talented and had a good family history
- ... but they didn't just choose her to survive. they chose her as a failsafe. a seed for a future contra-revolution. someone who could restart the discipline. someone who could lead a second uprising when the time was right
- she didn't ask for this. she was 17 and grieving and they put a future on her shoulders before they died for her
- scattered [[a vârsălhe|vârsalhos]] still survive elsewhere, in hiding. some of them still think she'll come back. they're waiting. she has letters she's never answered. she burns them now without reading
- she's a potential figurehead. and the people who rally at her name want çintalhense blood
- she would be killed if the [[çintalhenses]] found her out. she could easily be the center of a violent contra-revolution
- "drifted" for a few years. eventually found the circus. its message was the exact opposite of what she'd been taught (and that's why she stayed)
- she still [[a vârsălhe|vârsalhas]] alone at night. it's all she has left of the discipline, the family and the world that raised her.

#### THE SELF-HATRED

- feels like a fraud: she performs joy she doesn't feel, hates herself for the dishonesty
- past sin: she killed a [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]]. nobody at the circus knows. she could have refused to kill them, chosen not to do the deed. means her training made killing into a kindness
- knight in a world with no knights: trained for a discipline that doesn't exist anymore
- she joined the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] partly to atone, partly to punish herself by surrounding herself with the people she's afraid of being seen by
- the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] trusts her completely — because she arrived clean of [[the betrayal]], not because they know her. she wants to be seen and punished; instead she was handed exemption. the one thing she can't bear: unearned, total trust, built on an accident of timing, protecting the worst secret in the troupe

#### FEELINGS TOWARD ÇINTALHENSES

- guilt is the dominant one. she killed one, and now she works with them. every interaction is haunted, pained
- shame about her old beliefs (she once thought [[çintalhenses]] were a threat to humanity. she has to live with having believed that, and with what her family did)
- instinctual fear she hates herself for. flinches when one moves suddenly near her. punishes herself for the flinch by training harder
- reverence she feels guilty over: she finds their transformations beautiful
- she wants to deserve to be near them
- she joined the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] partly to be caught. she wants to be seen and she wants to be punished. proximity to [[çintalhenses]] is both at once
