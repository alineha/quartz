aldvor pôsc. a normal man, poor, caught at his wit's end. he converted.

big, chubby, short. blonde. clean-shaven. the kind of body that gives good hugs and reads as uncomplicated.

born ~49 PÇ. urban working class. [[morrânça]]'s outer terraces. brihčian-speaking household pre-revolution

raised [[căntalume]]. lapsed in his teens. found it again as an adult.

### THE CRAFT

- shoemaker's family. cobblers, leatherworkers, tailors. people who fix worn things. generations of mending what others would discard
- the changing times broke the trade. cheap manufactured goods undercut craft. the family business couldn't make ends meet. by his late teens it was over
- he kept doing the work anyway. on his own time. for friends, for neighbours, for nobody — the practice of the hands continued past the economic viability of the trade. he was, in his trade, a tiny pre-revolution version of what [[orvaccio]] runs now. taking the worn-out, the discarded, restoring them to use

### THE LOST PARENTS, THE TEENAGE YEARS

- lost both parents young. mid-teens.
- the years after were rough. he wasn't violent — he was never violent — but he was unkind
- the specific shame: a kind aunt took him in for a while. tried to help. he was sixteen and full of grief. eventually he took money from her and vanished. left her worse off than she had been before she tried to be good to him
- not theft from a stranger. theft from a person who had loved him. the betrayal of kindness offered
- he never undid it. couldn't. didn't try to find her later because the shame was too big. she may still be alive.
- he put himself back together over the years that followed. the practice of being kind began as penance. the pickle-eating, hug-giving, cheering-up man is a twenty-year-long apology to one person aldvor will never see again
- [[vow]] knew the story. he told her. she loved him anyway. she didn't fall for a kind man. she fell for a man who had been unkind once and had spent twenty years being kind. she fell for the practice

### THE PICKLES PRINCIPLE

_the meme: he asked for no pickles_. they came with pickles. he ate the pickles. he would not get the worker who forgot in trouble. he would eat the pickles even if he was allergic

- he absorbs minor harm so others won't be punished. reflex, not strategy. as automatic as breathing
- this is what made him the perfect victim of the battery system. the man who'd eat the pickles is the man who'd sign a contract that destroys him so his family's debts are paid, and never name what was done to him
- and it's what broke [[vow]]. he was being kind to his own guards. making puns for the people draining him. his ongoing kindness in conditions designed to crush kindness is what broke her frontier doctrine. softness gets you killed, her parents said. and here was a man whose softness was outlasting the killing of him. the contradiction is the entire revolution

### THE PERSON (BEFORE)

- observant. saw emotion in micro-expression. asked about it
- noticed when people felt bad. tried to cheer them up. when that didn't work he asked, plainly: is there anything i can do? he meant it
- a deflector of attention away from himself. you'd talk to aldvor for an hour and learn nothing about him, and he'd know your whole week
- self-deprecating in a warm way. aimed his jokes at himself to protect you from feeling bad about your own thing. you'd laugh and only realize later that he'd absorbed the shot
- the puns were mostly bad. he committed to a pun he knew was bad. delivered it anyway. waited for the groan. that was the joke
- physically affectionate. easy hugs. sat close. touched shoulders absent-mindedly when passing. the body comfort vow craved without knowing it
- kept few possessions. the few he had were cared for. oiled tools, mended clothes, repaired things. a man whose love language was repair
- could not stand confrontation. absorbed cost rather than raised it. when serious conflict was unavoidable he went quiet rather than fought. could be a vacuum in an argument — the absence of pushback that let the other person hear themselves

### THE GIFT (THE GLOVE)

- before they got involved, he gave [[vow]] something he'd made. small. functional. quiet
- a mended military glove. she'd torn the finger of one on patrol. he saw her throw it out, grabbed it, reattached the finger invisibly. refused payment. the glove still worked. better than the original, the finger held tighter than the manufacturer's stitch
- twenty-plus years later the glove is in vow's drawer at the apex. she has used it ten thousand times in her career. not on display. not visible. but kept
- it is the only thing the iron-general has from before she clawed out, and from after
- [[quell]] does not know it exists. losing the glove would destroy vow in a way she could not survive professionally

### MEETING VOW, LOVING VOW, BECOMING THE BREAK

(covered in [[vow]]'s sections and in [[the bookend]]. summary only here)

- battery on her roster at the apex draw-station. number on a sheet. then a name. then the funny man. then the man she could not justify draining
- loving aldvor and turning against the system were one act. seeing him was the treason
- they had [[silv]] (70 PÇ). aldvor was already converted; vow still unconverted. silv was born çintalhense. aldvor understood, while he was still sane, what he had passed on. believed he had ruined his son's chance at a normal life
- the guilt never resolved. his mind went before it could
- he doesn't know silv died. he still asks for him

### THE TRANSFORMATION

- gold wheels around his wrists and neck. floating. don't quite touch the skin. they look like shackles disguised as jewellery
- they don't move. they're part of him. the body has restructured around them
- they glow faintly. constantly. low warm gold
- they make a soft ring when he moves. quiet. almost musical. the care facility staff find it soothing. he doesn't notice it anymore
- the contact points — where the wheels meet the skin at wrist and neck — are rough. ugly. the transformation is incomplete there. the wheels sit on him like ill-fitting jewellery, mismatched, never quite his

### THE DESCENT, THE CARE

- mind began going years before the revolution. by the late 80s he was functionally absent in conversation
- [[vow]] kept him home for years. nurses. carers. the apex's resources poured into preserving the man inside the empire's most powerful woman's quarters. she fought it. she fought it for a long time
- it wore her. it wore the staff. by the late 90s he wandered. didn't know the apex. didn't know vow. didn't know which century the wind in the towers belonged to
- the decision to move him to a care home was the worst thing vow ever did to him deliberately. she signed the paperwork in the same hand that has signed thousands of military orders. she could not stop her hand from shaking. none of her staff have ever seen that hand shake
- the care facility is in the [[the periferia interna|periferia interna]]. not [[the apex]]. [[vow]] chose warmer ground for him. she could have afforded the cold-bright apex hospitals. she chose down. she put him where the unconverted poor would be near, where the singing he half-remembers would be in earshot, where his old life is closer
- [[quell]] has never asked her about that choice. it's the one decision of hers that was unguarded toward him. she did not place his father where the empire would see him. she placed him where the empire would not. the iron-general put her husband, the revolution's root, in the gentlest place she could find
- she visits rarely. when she does she is brief. she leaves before he sees her cry. she does not cry until she is back at the apex with the door closed

### THE PRESENT-DAY MAN

- usually calm. mostly here — in this room, in this moment, with whoever is in front of him being kind to him
- he assumes he must have done something to deserve the kindness he's receiving. he tries to be kind back. that's still working. the practice has outlasted the cognition
- the hands remember. given material — a worn glove, a torn cloth, a piece of leather — he mends. doesn't always remember finishing. doesn't always remember why he started. the work of fifty years of craft lives below the language he's losing
- the staff have learned to bring him things to fix. when he is fixing, he is the most peaceful man in the building
- he sings the [[căntalume]] hymns sometimes. better than he speaks. the singing of light-shapes outlasts the speaking. the volunteers cry occasionally and don't show it
- he asks for [[silv]]. not constantly. occasionally. "is silv coming today?" the nurses tell him gently that silv is busy. he accepts it. an hour later he might ask again
- he sometimes thinks [[quell]] is [[silv]]. this hurts [[quell]] every time and [[quell]] has never told [[vow]]. [[quell]] answers as silv when it happens. "yes, papa, i'm here." aldvor is delighted in those moments. quell carries a dead brother by pretending to be him for an hour every visit
- a moment of confusion occasionally turns to panic. the staff settle him. these are the worst moments — for [[vow]] when she visits, and for quell always
- he is rarely sad. mostly he is here, with whoever is in front of him
- he does not know aldvor-the-revolution exists.
- he doesn't know there are statues of his wife. he doesn't know what year it is most of the time. the only person in the empire whose mind has not been touched by the revolution. the last unconquered territory
