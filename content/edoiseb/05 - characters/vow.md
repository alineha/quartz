- not a villain necessarily... she's a true believer who watched [[çintalhenses]] being used as power sources and decided that had to end. her ideology is strong, and she's right about a lot of things
- initially brought on to the movement as a result of falling in love with a [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] man, [[aldvor]].
- they had a child, [[silv]]. the boy was born already a [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]]. it struck upon vow that she had to make the world better, both for him, and for his father.
- converted & fought in the revolution. killed many unconverted people. did the necessary violence to bring the new world into being and doesn't apologize for it
- her transformation is extremely "classical angel". wings, halos, the whole biblical-angel vibe. she's the face of the new state to many people. statues of her exist. she's used in propaganda
- [[quell]]'s wing-eyes are almost a quote of hers. his rebellion at the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] is literally a defacement of her image. family drama becomes a political mess!
- her name is almost never used (people fear her name). people at the circus say "his mother" or "the general." never "vow"

#### THE FRONTIER

- frontier military family. far province. a cold "nowhere". "onde judas perdeu as botas".
- grew up bilingual. vespernic at home, palâscian at school. she dropped vespernic deliberately when she clawed out. it surfaces only unguarded. never around [[quell]]
- harsh parents. doctrinal, not sadistic. they believed hardness was the only love that keeps a child alive. softness = a liability that gets you killed
- clawed out of the provinces. by 20, the youngest-ever high-ranking officer in [[morrânça]]. exceptional, but alone.

#### THE VOW

- "vow" predates the government. it's the promise she made clawing out. her actual, government name is vorelle.
- a vow to herself: never to be powerless again. never at anyone's mercy but her own
- it's the engine of the rise. "never powerless" is what produces the youngest-ever officer
- the state later reads "vow" as an oath to the revolution. it never was. it meant "no one but me". the regime built its iconography on a woman whose founding promise was pure self-preservation
- silv's death is the vow defeated. the one event that made her powerless again. she commanded a revolution and couldn't stop the death of someone under her protection.
- becoming her parents toward quell = clawing the power back the only way she knows. she cannot survive being powerless twice

#### THE STATION

- early 20s, rising officer. given command of a [[çintalhenses|battery]] detail at a strategically critical draw station. it powered [[the apex]] itself
- she commanded the installation — security, escort, schedules, quotas. the officer over the people-equipment
- handlers reported to her. she was the authority, not the hands
- she knew every battery on her roster as a line item. output. condition. projected service life

#### [[ALDVOR]]

- [[aldvor]] was a [[çintalhenses|battery]] on her roster. poor, contract-signed, drawn on schedule, declining on schedule
- the frontier conditioning was supposed to make this fine. it makes the suffering of batteries not register. for years, it works. he is a number that makes power
- then it fails. on him. for no defensible reason
- he's a funny man. makes puns. he loves. he keeps his soul, even at his wit's end. it's beautiful, and heartbreaking.
- she starts seeing him. once she sees one, the roster stops being just numbers on a sheet
- loving [[aldvor]] and turning against the system are not two events. they are one. seeing him is the treason. the love and the revolution have a single root: a failure of the hardness her parents installed
- kept him home for years before the care decision. the only military order she ever signed with a shaking hand. [[the periferia interna]] was a deliberate kindness. visits rarely. cries only at [[the apex]] with the door closed
- has a mended glove that was his first gift to her in a drawer.
