- small, gentle, low-stakes. nothing dangerous, ever. these creatures already survived the worst thing that could happen to them; the act will never ask them to risk anything
- routines: balancing tricks, recognition games, vaudeville stuff — a dog choosing a card, a crow doing counts, ensemble pieces where multiple animals do one small coordinated thing. half the comedy is animals refusing to cooperate and [[vesnê]]'s deadpan response. one of them falls asleep mid-routine. another wanders off. she just continues
- the audience laughs. they don't know what they're watching. [[orvaccio]]'s narration sometimes does, depending on the room
- the menagerie has two pipelines:
  - the old guard — pre-revolution survivors. household pets converted by their owners with the cheap kits to function as portable chargers. ten-plus years old. dying off one by one. [[vesnê]] has had some of them since the beginning. one or two named, beloved, ancient. when one dies the troupe grieves a creature that survived more than they did
  - the byproducts — official [[the academy terrace|conversion-ring]] castoffs. lab animals the state didn't bother to document. they exist. the state has no use for them. some make their way out — through sympathetic workers, [[cinzária]] smuggle-paths, [[corda]]'s networks. [[vesnê]] is known. people who find a wrong-bodied dog or a too-still cat know where to send it
- her menagerie is, technically, contraband. ongoing evidence of state cruelty walking around eating dinner. she and [[corda]] run this together. the budget fights are real and a cover for the rest of it

#### TOTÓ AND VÉU

- totó. the old dog. pre-revolution survivor. has been with [[vesnê]] since the early post-revolution years. ancient. half-blind. his transformation is small, only a faint constant glow under the fur. mostly sleeps. occasionally toddles into a routine and is loved for it.
- véu. the corvid. younger. transformation: extra-glossy, sharp; pupils that catch [[lutrons|lutron]]-shimmer the way [[vesnê]]'s do. clever in the unsettling way corvids are, plus a little more. does the counting tricks, the recognition games, the precise small acts. the act's actual workhorse
- véu is also a witness. a creature that watches the troupe and may know more than anyone realizes.
