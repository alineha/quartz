#### CHILDHOOD

- 7-10 years ago = revolution. quell was 10-15 then. now early-to-mid 20s
- [[the battery era (25-89PÇ)|pre-revolution]] (age 0-7): his mother unconverted, fighting in the resistance that became the revolution. barely saw her. raised by extended family/servants/whoever was around. he learned to read her moods early because they were unpredictable
- her conversion (age 7-10): she chose to become a battery as part of her revolutionary work, to get inside the system, build the network, organize from within. he watched her body change. watched her come home foggy and slow after shifts. she'd not recognize him for hours sometimes. he was scared of her then.
- the [[the revolution (90PÇ)|revolution]] itself (age 10-12): she was gone for weeks at a time. he didn't know if she'd come back. once she did and was missing part of an arm (replaced, in transformation, with something else)
- the new world (age 12-16): she rose fast. they moved up. suddenly they were aristocracy. she was always working. when she was home, she performed motherhood the way kindle performs joy— as a result of duty, not feeling. he doesn't know to this day if she loves him or just thinks she should
- his conversion (age 16): his mother arranged it as his birthday gift. the best thing she could give him: citizenship in the new world she built. he said yes because he loved her and wanted her approval and didn't know yet that he could want different things. he can't separate his consent from his desire-for-her-approval. did he choose this, or did he choose her?
- how [[silv]] plays into the conversion: [[vow]] converting quell "as a gift" at 16 is also [[vow]] making sure the surviving son is at least one of the people the [[the revolution (90PÇ)|revolution]] was for. she lost the born-[[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] son. she would not be left with a merely-unconverted one. quell's conversion lives in [[silv]]'s shadow. quell may not fully understand this
- adolescence as [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] (16-19): groomed for military or politics or both. good at it superficially. something didn't fit. started missing his old eyes. started realizing his lutron-sense made him part of a network he hadn't asked to join
- the break (19-22ish): small rebellions first. eventually found the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] (attended a show, met someone, asked to join). his mother thought it was a phase. then he stayed. now estranged but not fully cut off. she sends agents. she sends letters. he doesn't answer

#### DOES HE LIKE HIS MOTHER?

- **he loves her.** he doesn't want to. she's the only parent he's ever had and she fought a revolution for people like him and she's terrifying and she's... his mother
- **he doesn't like her.** being around her is exhausting. she sees him as a project, a continuation, a piece of her work. she doesn't ask what he wants. she informs him what's good for him
- **he admires her.** her work is real. her revolution was right. the world she built is better in measurable ways. he can't argue with what she's done. he just believe it could be even better if everyone could get along
- **he resents her.** she gave him a body he didn't fully choose. she put him in rooms he didn't ask to be in. she's so good at being right that he can never quite name what's wrong
- **he's afraid of her.** not physically (she's never hurt him), but she has the power to destroy his life if she decides his rebellion has gone too far. and he isn't sure where her line is. so he watches himself around her. he edits himself. she's the only person in his life he can't be fully himself in front of
- the parallel: the two people quell can't perceive are [[kindle]] (unconverted) and [[vow]] (withheld on purpose). he's drawn to [[kindle]] partly because the experience echoes his mother, but with [[kindle]], the inaccessibility feels like an invitation. with his mother, it's a wall. that's why one feels like love and the other feels like grief
- his obsession with [[kindle]] is partly an inheritance. he learned to fixate on inaccessible people from his mother. he's repeating a pattern he doesn't see
- late-stage realization (when [[kindle]]'s reveal happens): part of what shatters is the fantasy that he's escaping his mother by loving [[kindle]]. because [[kindle]] (daughter of pre-revolution war criminals) would be the perfect figure to make his mother explode. quell has to face that part of his love is just pointed at his mother. he didn't pick [[kindle]] in spite of her. he picked her partially because of her. the real work is learning to love [[kindle]] for [[kindle]], not for what she means against his mother
- quell was at the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] before [[the betrayal]]. he cannot prove he wasn't the leak. we know he wasn't. he - the private dread: did he say something? to his mother's people, an agent he didn't clock, a letter he answered once? he edits himself for [[vow]] constantly — what if the edit failed once and he never noticed
- he can't be sure his own innocence is real, the way he can't be sure his conversion was his choice. another thing done to him that he can't locate the seam of
- the symmetry with [[kindle]]: she is provably innocent of the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]]'s crime and secretly guilty of a worse one. he is innocent in fact and cannot prove it even to himself. inverse betrayer-shapes. drawn to each other

#### WHY KINDLE (NOT JUST ANY UNCONVERTED)

- ALL unconverted are invisible to [[lutrons|lutron]]-sense - that's not unique to her. but [[kindle]] is the first unconverted person he's spent prolonged time around as an equal. his upbringing kept him in [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] circles. he was always going to fixate on someone unconverted.... [[kindle]] is just the one he happened to meet
- she doesn't perform for him. every other unconverted is careful with him because his mother could destroy them. [[kindle]] treats him with polite-careful distance like all [[çintalhenses]], but doesn't curry favor. for him this reads as honesty. it's actually fear. he doesn't know that
- she's good at the things he can't do. swordwork, physical mastery, presence in a body. his transformation is advanced enough that his coordination has shifted. he can't move the way she moves. unconverted people get to keep being competent at being human bodies, and he's losing that. [[kindle]] is the most striking example he's ever seen
- she's sad and he can tell. [[lutrons|lutron]]-sense doesn't work on her, but his wing-eyes read her through every other channel (micro-expressions, posture, the way she holds her sword between acts, the way she laughs at things that aren't funny). he reads her like a book. she doesn't know she's a book
- why quell specifically (and not the other çintalhenses she works with) breaks her performed self: he's the only one obsessed. the others have [[lutrons|lutron]]-sense for each other, so most of their attention lives inside that network. [[kindle]] is in their peripheral vision at best. quell has no [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] network to occupy him at the [[l'cirqur lhêvora|circus]] — he's an outsider here too. all his attention is free. all of it lands on her

#### THE OBSESSION (LOVE, PROJECTION, OR POSSESSIVENESS?)

- all three, at different points in time!
- starts as projection. he doesn't know her. he sees her sadness and makes up a story (a tragic knight, a noble soul, someone he can save). it's not real. it's a romance he's authoring
- changes to possessiveness. wants to know her, but also wants to be the only one who knows her. jealous. uses class privilege to make himself indispensable (paying for her equipment, smoothing over circus problems his mother could cause). corrosive
- could (and will!) become love. requires him to want [[kindle]] as she is rather than kindle as he's imagining her
