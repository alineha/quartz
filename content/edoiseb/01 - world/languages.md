TWO main language families in the southern hemisphere: **lumenal** and **vespernic**. historically unrelated, no shared roots.

a third — **marégan** — spoken in the [[maréga states]]. tropical, isolated, polytheistic.

the three [[palâscia|palâscian]]-region religions are lumenal-language artifacts. [[căntalume]], [[vitravora]], [[éclisara]] are lumenal words.

### LUMENAL FAMILY

the **dominant family**. soft consonants, vowel-rich, frequent diacritics (â, ê, ô, í, ç), nasalized endings (-ão, -õ, -en), characteristic -ccio / -elle / -arèn / -orre endings. warm. lyrical. ornamental

- palâscian ([[palâscia]]) — the imperial standard. spoken across the empire as the administrative tongue. names like _quelisâre_, _orvaccio_, _vesnê_ are characteristically palâscian. the state has been aggressively standardizing it post-revolution. accelerated language death in the conquered regions.
- brihčian ([[palâscia|brihčiá]]) — the older name for palâscian. what was spoken before the revolution renamed everything. mostly the same language with vocabulary the state has scrubbed. words for things the new world doesn't want anymore. brihč is brihčian. palâscia is palâscian. older unconverted speak with brihčian habits — _different word for "home"_, _different word for the wind_, slower cadence. the resistance speaks brihčian-inflected palâscian and outsiders don't notice. kindle's parents spoke brihčian
- andrescan ([[andresca]]) — sister language. mountain register. harder consonants, more glottal stops (closer to vespernic in sound but historically and grammatically lumenal). spoken at home; palâscian in school. a country forced to live in two languages. names with _-eska_, _-ana_, _-on_, _-gar_ endings. fewer diacritics
- lhiratan ([[lhirâta]]) — coastal sister. cosmopolitan. influenced by sea trade — has borrowed vocabulary from distant ports ([[maréga states]] included). less divergent from palâscian than andrescan. wealthier, more resentful — close enough to be partly absorbed, rich enough to remember being distinct. names with _-ata_, _-ino_, _-veril_, _-mara_ endings
- kalestian ([[kalesta]]) — the client state's tongue. very close to palâscian; they were already partly culturally aligned pre-revolution. _functionally a dialect_, though kalestians call it a language and palâscians call it a dialect. the argument matters politically
- brâhomi ([[brâhom]]) — sister, rural, slightly more inflected. has a separate set of vocative-case word forms used in calling out — to people, to livestock, to the dead (matches the brâhomi religious blend of éclisara + harvest religion). names with _-im_, _-ren_, _-aldo_, _-osa_ endings

### VESPERNIC FAMILY

harder consonants, fewer vowels, frequent consonant clusters (kr-, sk-, vh-, mn-) that look impossible to palâscian eyes. umlauts (ä, ö, ü) instead of acutes when diacritics appear. word-endings tend to be -en, -or, -ar, -gn, -t. stoic, sparse, less ornamental

- vespernic — spoken in [[vesperna]] and the [[high south]]. the only conquered region where palâscian is a genuinely foreign language and not a sister tongue. this is why [[vesperna]]'s annexation is incomplete — language barrier is part of why the state can only hold cities, not the interior. the vespernic interior is where people flee to disappear, and they disappear partly because nobody from [[palâscia]] can follow the conversations
  - names like _halben_, _aldvor_, _vesperna_ are vespernic in feel.
  - [[vow]]'s frontier family was vespernic-adjacent in the provinces. [[vow]] grew up bilingual — vespernic at home, palâscian at school. she dropped vespernic deliberately when she clawed out. it's not in her professional voice. it surfaces only in the rare unguarded moment — and never around [[quell]]
  - [[bell]]'s mother tongue is vespernic. they kept it. they think in vespernic. they translate everything into palâscian to speak to the troupe. this is part of why [[bell]] is quiet — palâscian is, for them, work. vespernic was home
  - vespernic culture is stoic, sparse, less ornamental than lumenal.

### THE MARÉGAN WILDCARD

a third root. unrelated to either family. tropical, polytheistic regions. their language doesn't map cleanly onto lumenal religious vocabulary — which is why maréga's religious obsession with the visions is so much milder. they don't have the same words to argue about

### THE CROSS-CULTURAL CONTEXT

the [[broken regions]] speak everything badly. vespernic and lumenal mixed, decayed, fragmenting. nobody fully shares a tongue. communication breakdown is part of the chaos

[[vesperna]]'s religion is folk [[éclisara]] — but the word they use is vespernic, not lumenal. the doctrine arrived; the vocabulary is theirs. [[éclisara]] is what palâscians call it. in vesperna it has a different name-- [[éclisara|finsteni]]

the [[high south]] is unconverted and speaks an older form of vespernic.

### NAMING CONVENTIONS (REFERENCE)

**palâscian**: -ccio, -elle, -arèn, -inha, -aldo, -orre, -une. diacritics common. ex: orvaccio, tobelle, silvarèn, quelisâre

**andrescan**: harder, mountain-feel. -eska, -ana, -on, -gar. fewer diacritics

**lhiratan**: trade-influenced. -ata, -ino, -veril, -mara

**kalestian**: nearly palâscian. similar endings, mild local variation

**brâhomi**: rural, vocative-rich. -im, -ren, -aldo, -osa

**vespernic**: harder consonants, fewer vowels. -en, -or, -ar, -gn, -t. umlauts where diacritics appear. ex: halben, aldvor, vesperna

**marégan / unconquered**: unknown
