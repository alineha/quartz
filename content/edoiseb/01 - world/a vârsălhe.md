- not "the order of X", just "a vârsălhe". members don't think of it as an institution, they think of it as a way of life. outsiders ask "what's that?" and members shrug
- a [[vitravora|vitravoran]] order in everything but name.
- masters teach apprentices. they live lives of discipline, meditation, formal duels, ceremonial work.
- swords are kept as a heritage even after guns made them militarily obsolete (real-world parallels include kendo, fencing, etc.)
- not learnt for combat, but for _gnosis_. training not in fight, but in _seeing_. the body's discipline and the knowledge of one's own body shortens your path towards [[vitravora|vitória]]. every form, performed correctly with the breath, is a measurable advance. breath by breath, closer
- in-world language:\
  a vârsălhe = the discipline itself\
  um vârsalho / uma vârsalha = a member\
  os vârsalhos = the members collectively\
  vârsalhar = to practice it (verb). kindle still vârsalhas alone at night

#### the meditation

- [[vitravora|vitravoran]] to the bone. mind blank, breath steady, body in form. the practice is the prayer
- the goal: the clearing. the state of mind ready to perceive without distortion. you reach it briefly. then it's gone. you reach it again
- a still mind is a mind that doesn't add to what it sees
- every form done well is _gnosis_ added to humanity's collective ascent. one practitioner, one breath, one breath closer to [[vitravora|vitória]]. the discipline is, doctrinally, useful work for the world's soul

#### the forms

- seven. each with a principle. one line, the form's gnostic kernel. the practitioner says it (silently or murmured) as they perform.
- the form of **disturbance** — _what disturbs the still tells you what is there._ you read a fight by what doesn't fit. a shift in stance, a held breath, a wrong silence. the form trains you to notice
- the form of **persistence** — _the small heat outlasts the great fire._ the form of endurance. of what refuses to go out. of finishing what you started long after the moment for it has passed.
- the form of **change** — _nothing is the same twice. trust the next move, not the last._ continuous adjustment. the form's whole purpose is to break the practitioner's habit of repetition. a vârsalha who does this form correctly cannot do it the same way twice in a row
- the form of **waiting** — _the gate teaches what the room cannot._ the form that trains the practitioner to inhabit the hesitation rather than rush past it
- the form of **remainder** — _what is left is what was true._ the bare form. practiced with no gear, no master, no formal space -- the form a vârsalha can still do when everything has been taken from them. (the form the [[cinzária]] vârsalhos practice. all they have)
- the form of **exposure** — _in full light there is nowhere to hide. neither for you nor for them._ the form of total visibility. uncomfortable. cruel. the discipline forbids skipping it
- the form of **arrival** — _the worst comes. meet it. do not be elsewhere._ the form for the moment that cannot be avoided. trains the body to be present at impact -- not flinching, not dissociating, not having already fled in the head. the opposite of fight-or-flight: staying

#### the daily practice

- **dawn:** wakefulness. clearing the mind so the day's experience can be received without distortion. shorter. faster. forward-facing
- **dusk:** retention. translating the day's perceptions into the body, so the mind sleeps clear. longer. slower. reflective
- both, daily, without exception, for a real vârsalha. the rhythm is the discipline. miss too many and the body forgets

#### the duels

- before a formal duel, both vârsalhos say _"i see you."_ a commitment. i am about to fight you fully, with disciplined attention, treating you as real. not a target — a seen person
- after, win or lose, both fighters say it: _"i was seen."_ acknowledgment that the duel was witnessed gnosis. the duel itself was the practice. the outcome is secondary
- these are the only fixed words in the discipline. everything else is principle and breath

#### the blades

- two. traditional pair. a longer one and a shorter one
- learned by the body individually. a vârsalha knows their blades the way a musician knows an instrument. you don't share them
- when the master considers the apprentice ready: either gifts them their blades, commissions them, or guides the apprentice in forging them. the blades' provenance is part of the vârsalho's history
