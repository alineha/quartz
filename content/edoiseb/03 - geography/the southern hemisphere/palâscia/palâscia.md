- country: officially the palâscia empire. originally, brihčiá (a name that is still used by older people and the resistance)
- brihčiá means "the wind place". it comes from brihč, a low wind/brisa. the country was historically known for being very windy
  - ##### THE WIND (BRIHČ)
  - the country was named for it. the brihč is a constant low wind that comes up the mountainside and through [[morrânça]]'s terraces. it makes the city hum
  - [[the battery era (25-89PÇ)|pre-revolution]], the brihč was loved. songs, poems, lullabies built around it. love poems comparing a lover's voice to the brihč. the country's entire self-image
  - [[post-revolution]], this is dying. the new self-image is militaristic, stone
  - [[quell]] associates the wind with home and childhood. cold, wind, the apex. when he's away from [[morrânça]], he misses the brihč the way you miss a heartbeat
  - [[kindle]] associates the wind with home too, but a warmer wind. pre-[[çintálhi]] the region she lived in had a softer, warmer brihč.
- palâscia is the post-revolution rename. military-imperial branding ("the empire of the central palace").
- people: the palâscians (official), the brihčiános (historical, resistance)

#### temperature as politics

- [[lutrons|lutron]] flow generates cold.
- [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] bodies run colder than unconverted bodies. core temperature a few degrees lower. they don't warm up through exertion the way humans do
- unconverted skin is shockingly warm. [[çintalhenses|çintalhense]] skin is unnervingly cool. [[kindle]] touches [[quell]] and her hand is a brand. [[quell]] touches [[kindle]] and she shivers from the cold
- [[lutrons|lutron]]-lit homes are colder than unlit ones. in winter, the unconverted poor sometimes turn off their [[lutrons|lutron]]-lights to stay warm. they sit in the dark to not freeze
- [[çintalhenses]] don't mind the cold. they don't prefer it but aren't bothered. unconverted people in the empire are always layered up. furs are everywhere. winter clothing trade is huge
